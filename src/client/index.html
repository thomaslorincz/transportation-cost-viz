Transportation Cost Visualization

Description

This visualization shows household transportation cost predictions in the Edmonton Central Metropolitan Area according to 3 city plans.

Each drawn dot represents 1 household. Due to the large volume of data, only 50% of households per scenario are shown (a uniformly random sample).

The data is produced for the entire CMA, but only Edmonton and St. Albert data is being considered because residential parcel boundary files are not yet available for other regions.

Now Scenario (2020) is based on current population and transportation trends. It includes 431,380 households (215,690 shown).

Business as Usual Scenario (2065) is a prediction of the CMA in 2065. It includes 711,158 households (355,579 shown).

City Preferred Scenario (2065) is a prediction of the CMA in 2065. It includes 809,922 households (404,961 shown).

Analysis

To calculate monthly transportation cost for each household, several daily trip and household data points are compounded:

  • The average cost of maintaining 1 vehicle is set to $3750/year.
  • The cost per kilometer travelled is $0.225. 17.5 cents for fuel + 5 cents for depreciation and marginal insurance.
  • On average, 330 will require vehicle travel (if a vehicle is used for commuting).
  • On average, 300 will require transit travel (if transit is used for commuting).
  • The average cost for a vehicle license is $25 per person.
  • Transit fare is $3 (without a transit pass).
  • Transit pass cost varies by person age:
    • Preschool age or younger: Free
    • Elementary age: $560/year
    • Junior High age: $560/year
    • Senior High age: $560/year
    • Post-Secondary age: $800/year
    • Work/Adult age: $1200/year
    • Senior/Elder: $135/year
